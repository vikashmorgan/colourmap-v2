# Question Bank

Use these prompts in batches. Select only what is relevant.

## Problem and User

- Who is the primary user and what job are they trying to get done?
- What is painful about the current way they solve this?
- How frequently does this problem happen?
- What happens if this problem is not solved?
- Which user segment matters most for the first release?

## Outcomes and Success

- What does success look like in 30/60/90 days?
- Which metrics define success (adoption, revenue, retention, time saved)?
- What is the minimum useful version that creates real value?
- What outcomes matter more than feature completeness?

## Product Scope

- Which 3 capabilities are must-have for v1?
- What is explicitly out of scope for v1?
- Which workflows must feel excellent from day one?
- What edge cases are unacceptable to fail?

## Feel and Experience

- How should the product feel (fast, calm, premium, playful, serious)?
- Which products inspire the desired interaction style?
- What must users feel in the first 60 seconds?
- What accessibility requirements are mandatory?

## Constraints

- What is the target launch date and what is fixed vs flexible?
- Who is building this and what skills are available?
- What systems must integrate (APIs, data stores, auth providers)?
- What compliance, privacy, or security boundaries exist?
- What budget or tooling constraints should guide stack choices?

## Technical Direction

- Do you prefer speed-to-market or long-term robustness right now?
- How much future scale should v1 anticipate?
- What level of reliability is required at launch?
- Which tech stack constraints are non-negotiable?

## Delivery and Operations

- How will work be split into milestones?
- What must be testable in each milestone?
- How will releases be validated before production?
- What observability is needed to detect failures quickly?

## Risk and Decisions

- What assumptions are riskiest right now?
- Which unknowns could delay execution most?
- What decision is needed next to unblock planning?
- What is the fallback if the preferred approach fails?
